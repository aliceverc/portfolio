Yoga Flyer Redesign

Skills: Illustrator, InDesign, typography (micro and macro), colour theory and use of white space.

Print Product - Redesign
For this project, the objective was to redesign a newspaper flyer for a yoga studio, adhering to specific typographic and layout guidelines. The task emphasized improving microtypography and visual hierarchy while maintaining the original dimensions of 136 mm x 100 mm, adaptable to either portrait or landscape format.

Design Approach:
  • Logo Recreation and Enhancement: The yoga studio's logo was meticulously recreated with minor refinements to improve visual appeal and ensure better integration within the flyer layout.
  • Typography and Microtypography: Focused on optimizing font sizes, spacing, and alignment for enhanced readability. Hierarchical text elements were established to guide the reader’s eye through the key information, such as free trial sessions and the yoga class schedule.
  • Visual Enhancements: Selected calming and professional imagery to complement the yoga theme, aligning with the flyer’s overall aesthetic and improving its appeal to potential participants.
  • Color Palette: Opted for a balanced and soothing color scheme that resonates with the themes of relaxation and well-being associated with yoga.

Constraints and Challenges:
  • The flyer had to be designed for newspaper print in CMYK mode, requiring careful attention to color contrast and legibility.
  • The layout had to balance textual content and visual elements within the constrained dimensions, ensuring an uncluttered, professional appearance.
  • Achieving a consistent and harmonious design while adhering to the provided manuscript text was essential.

Outcome: The redesigned flyer achieved a clean, professional look that effectively communicates the studio’s offerings while being visually appealing to the target audience. Key information, such as the schedule and contact details, was highlighted through strategic use of typography and layout. The result was a functional and aesthetic flyer ready for newspaper publication.
French Renaissance Antiqua Typography Poster

Skills: Typography, visual communication, information design, layout composition and historical research.

Print Product - Infographic
For this project, I developed an educational poster exploring the French Renaissance Antiqua typeface class, a foundational typographic style originating during the Renaissance era (1400–1600). The objective was to design a visually engaging and informative piece that highlights the historical evolution, distinguishing features, and notable examples of this typeface class.

Design Approach:
  • The poster utilized a structured grid layout to organize detailed content, including the typeface’s etymology, historical significance, and visual characteristics.
  • A sophisticated color palette with subtle contrasts ensured readability and aesthetic harmony.
  • Iconographic representations and carefully selected examples, such as Bembo and Garamond, provided visual references for the typeface’s unique qualities.

Content Highlights:
  • Explored the Renaissance cultural movement's influence on typography, emphasizing Aldus Manutius' pivotal contributions.
  • Detailed the hallmark features of French Renaissance Antiqua, such as slanted roof strokes in minuscules, rounded serif transitions, and an emphasized contrast in stroke weight.
  • Included an annotated visual breakdown of key letterforms to guide recognition of this typeface style.


Key Challenges and Solutions:
  • Synthesizing complex historical and technical information into an accessible format required careful content prioritization and clarity.
  • Balancing educational depth with visual appeal was achieved through typographic hierarchy and strategically placed imagery.

Skills: Typography, visual communication, information design, layout composition, and historical research.

BeeAware App

Skills: User research, persona creation, empathy mapping, customer journey mapping, low- and high-fidelity wireframing, interactive prototyping, mockup design, and user-centered UI/UX design principles.

Dummy Application
The BeeAware app was conceptualized and designed as a comprehensive tool to raise awareness about pollinator conservation and empower users to take actionable steps towards sustainable ecological practices. With an engaging and user-centered approach, the app targets diverse audiences, including families, eco-conscious individuals, and community gardeners.

UX Design Process:
  • User Research:Conducted detailed surveys and interviews to identify target users and their specific needs, resulting in the creation of three primary personas:
    • A child passionate about nature and eager to learn through interactive games.
    • A home gardener seeking practical tips to make their garden pollinator-friendly.
    • A sustainability enthusiast interested in recipes, natural remedies, and broader ecological issues.
  • Empathy Mapping: Mapped users’ emotions, thoughts, and challenges to gain a deeper understanding of their motivations and pain points. These insights directly influenced the app’s key features and content structure.
  • Customer Journey Mapping: Developed detailed journey maps to visualize how users would interact with the app across different touchpoints, ensuring a seamless and engaging experience from onboarding to content exploration.

UI Design Process:
  • Wireframes:
    • Low-Fidelity Wireframes: Focused on layout, structure, and navigation flow to test early usability concepts.
    • High-Fidelity Wireframes: Added detailed visual elements, typography, and initial color schemes to validate the app's aesthetic and functional direction.
  • Mockups: Designed polished mockups showcasing the app's vibrant, eco-inspired color palette and clean, modern interface to reflect the theme of nature and sustainability.
  • Clickdummy Prototype: Created an interactive prototype to demonstrate user flows, including gamified activities, recipe-sharing features, and pollinator-friendly gardening tips. This prototype was essential for gathering feedback during usability testing.

Features and Highlights:
  • Educational Content: An extensive library of resources about pollinators, their ecological importance, and actionable tips for their preservation.
  • Gamified Learning: Interactive modules designed to educate children in a fun and engaging way.
  • Community Features: Options to share gardening experiences, ecological activities, and sustainable practices with like-minded users.
  • Accessibility: Design adheres to WCAG guidelines, ensuring inclusivity with clear typography, high contrast, and simple navigation.

Challenges and Solutions:
  • Diverse User Needs: Balanced the needs of varied user groups by providing tailored features for children, adults, and community users.
  • Complex Information Simplification: Transformed scientific data into easy-to-understand infographics and bite-sized content to maintain user engagement.
  • Iterative Design Improvements: Addressed feedback from usability testing to refine navigation, enhance feature clarity, and streamline workflows.


Skills: User research, persona creation, empathy mapping, customer journey mapping, low- and high-fidelity wireframing, interactive prototyping, mockup design, and user-centered UI/UX design principles.
